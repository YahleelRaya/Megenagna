Megenagna Employment Services Worker and Employer Agreement

• Employer has the right to hold the employee accountable for any damages they might have caused but only upon proof of fault in a court of law.

• The employee shall have a minimum tenure of one month, with full pay.

• There shall be a notice of 2 weeks given by either party upon desire to terminate the employment contract.

• The employer shall provide necessary tools and amenities (housing, resources, and supplies) for the worker to complete household tasks. The worker shall not be obligated to use their own money towards expenses that are the employer's responsibility.

• All pay shall be disbursed timely, be tracked in the Megenagna App, and be verified by both parties. If there is a delay, employers must communicate the situation with their workers and shall compensate fully what is owed.

• After the employment contract is terminated, if the employer has not paid the workers salary in full, they will be expected to clear up the balance within 2 months of the official contract termination date. If not, the employee reserves the right to take legal action to receive the owed amount.

• Upon the start of the employment period, a one-time fee of 5% of the intended employee salary shall be paid from the employer to Megenagna PLC. This fee shall not be deducted from the employee's salary and is a separate service fee intended for the commission services provided by Megenagna PLC.

• The commission fee is required regardless of how long the employment lasts.

The Responsibilities and Rights of Megenagna PLC

• Megenagna shall not be liable for damages whether emotional, mental, or physical (including property and body) caused by either party.

• Megenagna reserves the right to offer legal support to Workers and Employers who would like to bring their case to the court of law.

• If the commission fee is not paid in full within a month of the employment start date, regardless of whether the employment has been terminated, Megenagna reserves the right to take legal action to receive the necessary compensation.

• Megenagna has the responsibility to disclose all necessary and correct information about either party to their counterpart including legal name, address, date of birth, and reference information.

• Megenagna will only disclose information about clients (worker and employer) to law enforcement if and only if compelled by a warrant.

• Megenagna reserves the right to ban any employee or worker from the app if they have been shown to violate the terms and conditions.
